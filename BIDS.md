# Bids — Ring On Demand Plumbing

How to set max CPC. Two layers: a **basic bid for everything**, then **higher bids on the high-intent terms that actually generate calls.**

## 1. Basic bid — ALL keywords (every city)

Set **$12 max CPC** as the default on every keyword.
- It's near the breakeven line (~$100 payout ÷ ~6 clicks ≈ $16), so clicks stay profitable.
- Cheap job-specific long-tail (garbage disposal, faucet, shower valve, hose bib) can go **$8** if you want to trim — optional.
- Generic local terms (plumber near me, plumbing company, licensed plumber) — keep at **$11**, they're pricey and lower-intent.

> Head terms will show "below first page bid $35–97" — ignore it. We bid low on purpose and take the profitable volume.

## 2. HIGH bids — the call-drivers (high intent)

These are panic / high-ticket / ready-to-dial searches. **Bid $24–30 for the first 1–2 weeks** to buy calls + conversion data, then ratchet down as Quality Score matures (real CPC drops below these caps).

### Universal high-intent (all 8 cities) — bid ~$22
- `[emergency plumber near me]`
- `"24 hour emergency plumber"`
- `"same day plumber near me"`
- `"emergency plumber open now"`

## 3. The logic

- **Tier 1 (high bids above)** = buys the calls. Crisis + high-ticket = caller dials, doesn't research.
- **Tier 2 (basic $12)** = keeps cheap long-tail serving so you get volume at the cap.
- After ~15 calls, switch the campaign to **Maximize Conversions** and let Google bid — these manual caps are the cold-start kickstart.
- **Mobile bid adjustment +20–30%** on every campaign (emergency plumbing is mostly mobile).
